# Style Archetypes

## Use This File

Read this file when the user asks for a more specific visual personality beyond generic polish, or when the UI needs a clear stylistic direction without losing product-grade restraint.

## How To Use

- Choose one archetype before styling in detail.
- Let layout, component behavior, color, motion, and depth follow the same personality.
- Do not mix multiple archetypes unless the product genuinely has distinct zones with different roles.
- If the request is vague, prefer the calmest archetype that still fits the product context.

## Quiet Product Precision

- **Feeling:** calm, exact, understated, product-first
- **Best for:** admin tools, settings panels, internal platforms, serious SaaS
- **Color tendency:** crisp neutrals with one restrained accent
- **Density:** medium to high
- **Component behavior:** quiet defaults, crisp focus, very low drama
- **Motion personality:** short, tight, nearly invisible
- **Depth behavior:** mostly flat with selective overlay elevation
- **Avoid:** decorative gradients, loud glow, theatrical hover

## Premium SaaS Polish

- **Feeling:** refined, modern, trustworthy, slightly aspirational
- **Best for:** dashboards, product marketing surfaces, customer-facing app shells
- **Color tendency:** balanced neutrals with controlled accent presence
- **Density:** medium
- **Component behavior:** polished but restrained, stronger CTA hierarchy
- **Motion personality:** smooth and brief, with clearer overlay transitions
- **Depth behavior:** light surface layering and careful shadow use
- **Avoid:** fake luxury cues, over-gloss, excessive card float

## Crisp Enterprise Console

- **Feeling:** operational, clear, sober, efficient
- **Best for:** dense analytics, monitoring, B2B workflow products
- **Color tendency:** cool or neutral grays with highly disciplined semantic color
- **Density:** medium to high
- **Component behavior:** compact, aligned, decisive
- **Motion personality:** minimal and utility-first
- **Depth behavior:** very restrained; hierarchy comes from structure first
- **Avoid:** playful motion, oversized radii, consumer-app softness

## Dark Luminous Control

- **Feeling:** focused, technical, high-contrast, controlled
- **Best for:** AI tools, creative tools, data-heavy dark interfaces
- **Color tendency:** dark neutrals with highly localized luminous emphasis
- **Density:** medium
- **Component behavior:** calm resting states with rare high-value highlights
- **Motion personality:** smooth, fast, and subtle
- **Depth behavior:** surface luminance and edge definition lead; glow remains rare
- **Avoid:** neon everywhere, muddy shadows, glow as default styling

## Soft Editorial Minimalism

- **Feeling:** quiet, spacious, deliberate, content-led
- **Best for:** editorial tools, portfolios, reading-heavy products, premium onboarding
- **Color tendency:** restrained neutrals with very limited accent
- **Density:** low to medium
- **Component behavior:** light chrome, strong typography, low surface count
- **Motion personality:** gentle but brief
- **Depth behavior:** nearly flat, with sparse overlay separation
- **Avoid:** dashboard density patterns, busy controls, decorative effects

## Restrained Futuristic Interface

- **Feeling:** advanced, precise, slightly experimental, still controlled
- **Best for:** AI products, technical demos, next-gen tooling with strong brand personality
- **Color tendency:** dark or cool neutrals with one sharp luminous accent
- **Density:** medium
- **Component behavior:** clean geometry, selective high-value emphasis
- **Motion personality:** fast, crisp, and spatially coherent
- **Depth behavior:** sharp edge definition, limited glow, careful elevated focal points
- **Avoid:** sci-fi clichés, constant pulsing, decorative glass everywhere

## Quick Archetype Check

Before final output, check:

- Does the chosen archetype fit the product role and audience?
- Are color, motion, depth, and component behavior all expressing the same personality?
- Is the archetype strengthening the product, not overpowering it?
- Would the design still feel coherent if the accent intensity dropped?
